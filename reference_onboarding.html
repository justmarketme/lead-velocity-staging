    Quality Defined

    What "Qualified"
    Actually Means

    We're specific about this because vague definitions waste everyone's time.

    What Qualifies

    • Matches your target geographic area
    • Fits the demographic criteria we defined
    • Has expressed interest in the product type
    • Provided accurate, verified contact details
    • Opted in to receive communication
    • Is reachable and responsive

    What Doesn't

    • Cold contacts from purchased databases
    • Leads outside your service area
    • People who didn't opt in
    • Invalid or fake contact information
    • Tire-kickers with no real intent
    • Leads already contacted by others

    This definition protects your spend and ensures your time is spent on conversations that matter.

    Investment Reality

    Understanding
    Cost & Volume

    Before we discuss numbers, let's talk about what drives them.

    Why Lead Costs Vary

    Lead costs aren't arbitrary. They're driven by market demand, targeting specificity, product complexity, and geographic factors. A life insurance lead in Johannesburg costs differently than a commercial policy lead in a rural area. This is economics, not pricing games.

    Why Volume Matters More Than "Testing"

    Brokers often say, "Let me try 5 leads and see how it goes." Here's the problem: 5 leads tells you nothing. Statistical significance requires volume. Consistency drives conversion.

    The Real Cost Comparison

    Consider what you already spend:

    Hours at networking events for 1-2 prospects
    Marketing campaigns with unclear ROI
    Time on referrals that never close
    Opportunity cost of inconsistent flow

    The question isn't "what does a lead cost?"it's "what does inconsistency cost you?"
